Understanding the business

My read on Voltflo

From what I can see, Voltflo isn't just another CRM for solar companies. You're building a commercial engine that helps solar installers in Ireland handle leads better, close faster, and spend less time buried in admin. That's a real problem worth solving — and the Irish solar market right now, with SEAI grants pushing residential demand, is exactly the right time to own that space.

But the biggest opportunity I see for Voltflo right now isn't about adding more features. It's about tightening the revenue system on two fronts simultaneously — how Voltflo itself sells and grows, and how Voltflo's product helps solar installers stop leaking money in their own pipelines.

The real leverage for Voltflo isn't more features. It's tighter execution across the revenue stages that already exist — and that's exactly where I'd focus.

The Irish solar market is moving fast. The companies that win won't just have the best software — they'll be the ones whose customers close more deals and waste less time on admin.

The two-sided opportunity

Where RevOps compounds for Voltflo

When David asked about RevOps, I think he's really asking two things at once — and both matter equally.

Side A — Internal RevOps (For Voltflo)

How do we help David sell Voltflo to more solar companies in Ireland, faster, without needing to hire 10 more salespeople? This is about Voltflo's own pipeline — lead sources, outreach, conversion, and retention.

Side B — Product RevOps (For Solar Installers)

How do we make Voltflo's product so powerful that solar installers can't imagine running their business without it? This is about what Voltflo builds — features that directly solve revenue leaks for installers on the ground.

The companies that win in B2B software are the ones that align both sides.

If Voltflo grows its own pipeline efficiently AND delivers measurable revenue improvements to its customers, it creates a flywheel — more installs, more testimonials, lower churn, higher LTV. That's the goal.

Side A — Internal

Voltflo's own revenue engine

As Voltflo moves from early traction to scale, the revenue engine needs to move beyond what any founding team can manage manually. Here are the three areas I'd address first:

1
Centralised lead intelligence — know exactly where every lead comes from
▼
The problem
Leads coming in from LinkedIn, email outreach, referrals, events, and the website are all sitting in different places. There's no single view of which sources actually convert to paid customers.
What I'd build
A centralised database that pulls all lead sources into one place — every WhatsApp enquiry, every inbound call, every form submission, every referral — using Make or n8n as the integration layer with Airtable or a lightweight database at the centre. On top of that, an AI layer using Claude or GPT that lets the team ask plain-English questions like 'which lead source had the highest conversion last month' or 'how many leads came in from LinkedIn vs referrals this quarter' and get instant answers without building a dashboard nobody checks.
Outcome
The founding team can see exactly where to double down on outreach and where to cut wasted effort. Decisions based on real data, not gut feel.

2
AI prospecting agent — find the right solar firms before they find you
▼
The problem
High-value solar installers in Ireland are busy. Cold outreach that sounds generic gets ignored. Manually identifying which firms are growing and worth approaching takes hours.
What I'd build
An automated prospecting workflow using n8n and Python that monitors public sources — SEAI registered installer lists, Irish planning permissions, Companies Registration Office filings — to identify solar firms showing growth signals. It then triggers a personalised outreach sequence from the founding team's inbox that mentions something specific to that company, making it feel like research, not spam.
Outcome
The founding team receives a curated list of 10 to 15 warm prospects every Monday morning with a draft outreach email ready to send for each one. One click to approve, one click to send.

3
Pipeline discipline and stale deal detection
▼
The problem
Revenue systems break when the pipeline can't be trusted. Deals sit in 'Proposal Sent' for three weeks with no follow-up, and nobody notices until the prospect signs with a competitor.
What I'd build
Automated stale deal detection that flags any deal that hasn't moved in 72 hours and prompts the right action — a follow-up email draft, a call reminder, or an internal Slack alert. Stage-entry and stage-exit criteria enforced in the CRM so the pipeline always reflects reality. A simple Monday morning summary delivered by WhatsApp or email: 'Last week — 12 leads, 7 demos booked, 3 proposals out, 2 stuck. Here's what needs attention.'
Outcome
Voltflo's own win rate improves because nothing falls through the cracks. The team spends time selling, not chasing spreadsheets.

Side B — Product

Where Voltflo's product creates value that installers can't walk away from

The solar installer market in Ireland is growing fast, but the operational reality on the ground is still messy. Installers are managing leads on WhatsApp, sending quotes by email, chasing SEAI paperwork manually, and losing track of jobs mid-installation. Voltflo can own this problem entirely — but only if the product goes deeper than a nice interface.

Here are the specific areas where Voltflo's product can create the kind of value that makes installers never want to leave:

1
Lead qualification before a human ever picks up the phone
▼
The problem
Solar installers waste enormous time calling people who are just browsing or who have a completely unsuitable property — north-facing roof, heavily shaded, rented property. Every bad call costs 20 minutes and a lot of frustration.
What I'd build
An AI-assisted qualification layer that scores every incoming lead automatically based on what the homeowner tells you — Eircode, property ownership, roof orientation, monthly electricity bill, timeline, budget range. The system tags the lead as hot, warm, or not suitable before anyone touches it. A brief 'lead prep summary' is generated for the sales rep so when they do call, they already know the context.
Outcome
Installers call fewer leads but close a higher percentage of them. Sales rep time is spent on the right people. Rejection rate drops.
AI scoring logicEircode enrichmentn8n
2
AI sales agent — answering calls and handling missed calls automatically
▼
The problem
A homeowner calls at 7pm on a Friday. Nobody answers. They call another installer on Saturday morning and book with them. That lead is gone. This happens constantly.
What I'd build
A voice AI agent built using ElevenLabs with a natural Irish accent that handles inbound calls when the team is unavailable. It answers basic qualification questions, books survey appointments directly into the installer's calendar, and handles common objections around SEAI grants, payback periods, and battery compatibility. For missed calls, the agent automatically calls back within two minutes, explains who it is, and offers to book a callback with the human team. The agent knows what to say and — critically — what not to say. It never gives definitive grant amounts or technical guarantees. It keeps it warm, human, and helpful.
Outcome
Zero missed lead opportunities. Installers stop losing Friday evening and weekend enquiries to competitors. Booking rates improve significantly.

3
Proposal follow-up that runs itself
▼
The problem
A beautiful proposal goes out on a Tuesday. The homeowner says they'll think about it. The installer forgets to follow up. Three weeks later the deal is dead and nobody knows why.
What I'd build
An automated follow-up sequence that triggers the moment a proposal is sent. If there's no response after 48 hours, a plain-English follow-up goes out. At 5 days, a second message that mentions installation dates filling up. At 10 days, the rep gets a prompt with likely friction points based on the lead's profile — 'This homeowner mentioned they were waiting on a BER cert. Worth checking in on that specifically.' If a proposal has been open but not signed, the system flags it as a warm re-engagement opportunity.
Outcome
Installers stop losing quotes to silence. More proposals convert. Sales cycles shorten. The rep always knows what to say next.
MakeEmail / SMS automationClaude API
4
Clean handoff from signed contract to installation — no information lost
▼
The problem
The deal is signed. Everyone's happy. Then operations can't find the site survey photos. The SEAI grant submission is missing a utility bill. The install gets delayed. The homeowner starts to worry. The goodwill from the sale evaporates.
What I'd build
A standardised handoff checklist that is automatically created when a deal moves to closed-won. It pulls together everything operations needs — survey photos, roof dimensions, access notes, ID documents, utility bill, BER cert status — and flags exactly what's missing before the job is scheduled. Milestone-based customer updates go out automatically at each stage: grant submitted, grant approved, install date confirmed, install complete, commissioning booked. An AI summary of the deal context is generated for the operations team so they know exactly what was promised to the customer during the sale.
Outcome
Jobs start on time. SEAI submissions go in clean the first time. Customers feel looked after. Referrals increase. Rework and chasing drops significantly.

5
Revenue visibility — answers in seconds, not reports nobody reads
▼
The problem
Business owners don't have time to build reports or read dashboards. They want to know three things fast — where are my leads coming from, where are deals getting stuck, and how much revenue is actually in the pipeline right now.
What I'd build
A centralised intelligence database that consolidates all lead sources, pipeline stages, job statuses, and revenue data. On top of it, a conversational AI layer that lets the business owner ask questions in plain English: 'What's my total revenue from closed jobs this month?' or 'Which lead source had the best conversion rate last quarter?' or 'How many proposals are currently sitting unsigned?' — and get a straight answer in seconds. No dashboards to interpret. No spreadsheets to update. Just questions and answers.
Outcome
Installers running 20-plus jobs a month finally have real visibility. Business decisions get made on actual data. The product becomes something they genuinely rely on daily.
